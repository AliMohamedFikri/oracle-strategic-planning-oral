## About Oracle Corporation

Founded in 1977, Oracle is a global powerhouse in enterprise technology, known for its foundational role in modern data management and its significant shift to cloud computing.

### 1. History and Evolution

- **1979 Breakthrough:** Oracle launched V2, the first commercial Relational Database Management System (RDBMS) implementing SQL, establishing a global standard for business data management.  
- **The Application Era (2000s):** Expanded through acquisitions like PeopleSoft and Siebel to provide comprehensive ERP and CRM software.  
- **The Cloud Pivot (2010s):** Acquisition of Sun Microsystems marked a strategic shift to cloud computing with Oracle Cloud Infrastructure (OCI), competing with AWS and Azure.  

### 2. Core Business Pillars

Oracle's business is structured around four main areas:

1. **Cloud Infrastructure (OCI):** Provides scalable, high-performance computing, storage, and networking globally.  
2. **Cloud Applications (SaaS):** Offers Fusion ERP, Fusion HCM, NetSuite, and other cloud-based business applications for finance, HR, and supply chain management.  
3. **Database Technology:** The Oracle Database, a gold standard for mission-critical data management, known for reliability and performance.  
4. **Hardware and Systems:** Engineered systems and servers for integrated hardware/software solutions.
